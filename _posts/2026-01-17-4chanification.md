# What *is* Social Media
## Like
The *Like* is my personal favorite metric. It measures, well, if you like something. *Like*s give positive feed back to *The Algorithm^TM^* to show you more things . From a user-friendly perspective, this should have the most weight. However, the inverse is true for many of these Algorithms. 
## Share 
Sharing things is the most social part of social media. Saying to your friends, "Hey look at this," is the core to most of our social interactions in both the digital and analogue world. This goes in both a positive and negative direction, however. It can equally be, "Hey, look at this cool shit," as it can be, "Yo, check out this bullshit." This is the heaviest metric for Algorithms, which makes sense. However, this is the least common interaction on these platforms. What outperforms it by miles is the comment.
## Comment 
This is where it all kicks in. Have you ever had an argument on Facebook, Twitter, etc. that kept you coming back weeks later to try to finish? This is the open secret of social media "engagement."
