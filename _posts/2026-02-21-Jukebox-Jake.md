Every so often I scroll through instagram to see what my artist friends are up to, and by that I mean have The Algorithm<sup>TM</sup> push literally everything on me *except* my friends. And by pure happenstance, on January 31, I got an ad for Daniel Lopatin (of Oneohtrix Point Never and the first vaporwave artist) doing a music workshop through School of Song. I feared this would be a masterclass, which are bullshit, so I did a little research to make sure this wasn't a scam. When I found that this was an actual art workshop with homework, song shares, and community; I made the educated impulse decision to go through with it for $200. 

I now have a month's worth of notes of general abstract thinking, but what matters the most out of this has been discovering and developing an intellectually honest audentity (audio identity). I just spent at least 10 minutes each brainstorming my abstract and direct influences and inspirations, my specific tools and techniques, and on what I am and what I am not. I found that despite wanting to make abstract noise using modular synthasizers, something I have no experience in, my true audentity lies with my 20+ years of experience playing with bass guitars.  

But what I found that was even more valuable than that was confidence. Having professional musicians genuinely *love* what I make 

My best takeaway is developing my audentity. Before, it was getting suppressed by trying to superficially be like the artists I currently listen to, so it was freeing to now embrace the roots I've been running away from.

The breadth of genres, techniques, and perspectives were invaluable. The biggest key factor for me was that my impostor syndrome is now destroyed. Consistently, I received shockingly authentic high praise from people who I considered more talented and experienced than me. Meeting each other as semi-anonymous peers really levels the playing field in many ways.
